Water

(Our bodies are made up of 60-70% water.)

Water is essential for bodily functions, temperature regulation and digestion. Human consumption of water should be at least 2-3 litres of water. Water flushes out toxins, waste products and excess salt. Water covers 71% of the Earth's surface. Water is also a home to aquatic plants and animals. The statistics have an estimation of 80% of wastewater being untreated and 40% of global diseases are water-related. Water not only sustains our body's regulations but it also sustains the agriculture which provides us with food. Albert Einstein said "The next wold war will be about the last drop of water."
